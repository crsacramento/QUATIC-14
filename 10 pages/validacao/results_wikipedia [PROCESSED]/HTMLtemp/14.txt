      <h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">
        <span dir="auto">
          Book creator
        </span>
      </h1>
      <div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
        <div id="contentSub">
        </div>
        <div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">
          
					Jump to:					
          <a href="#mw-navigation">
            navigation
          </a>
          , 					
          <a href="#p-search">
            search
          </a>
        </div>
        <div id="mw-content-text">
          <p>
            <big>
              With the 
              <i>
                book creator
              </i>
               you can create a book containing wiki pages of your choice. You can export the book in different formats (for example PDF or ODF) or order a printed copy.
            </big>
          </p>
          <div style="margin: 10px 0;">
            <div class="collection-button ok">
              <a href="/w/index.php?title=Special:Book&amp;bookcmd=start_book_creator&amp;referer=Ibrahim+Ismail+Chundrigar+Road">
                Start book creator
              </a>
            </div>
            <div class="collection-button cancel">
              <a href="/wiki/Ibrahim_Ismail_Chundrigar_Road" title="Ibrahim Ismail Chundrigar Road" rel="nofollow">
                Cancel
              </a>
            </div>
            <div style="clear: both;">
            </div>
          </div>
          <p>
            <big>
              <i>
                See 
                <a href="/wiki/Help:Books" title="Help:Books">
                  Help:Books
                </a>
                 for more information.
              </i>
            </big>
          </p>
          <h2>
            <span class="mw-headline" id="Using_the_book_creator">
              Using the book creator
            </span>
          </h2>
          <p>
            After the 
            <i>
              book creator
            </i>
             has been enabled, the box as seen below is shown above each wiki page.

          </p>
          <table>
            <tbody>
              <tr>
                <td>
                  <div class="thumb tright">
                    <div class="thumbinner" style="width:714px;">
                      <a href="/wiki/File:Collection_Extension_-_Create_a_book_box.png" class="image">
                        <img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/7/79/Collection_Extension_-_Create_a_book_box.png" width="712" height="78" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="712" data-file-height="78"/>
                      </a>
                      <div class="thumbcaption">
                        <div class="magnify">
                          <a href="/wiki/File:Collection_Extension_-_Create_a_book_box.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge">
                            <img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.24wmf7/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt=""/>
                          </a>
                        </div>
                        The book creator interface
                      </div>
                    </div>
                  </div>
                </td>
              </tr>
            </tbody>
          </table>
          <dl>
            <dt>
              <i>
                Add this page to your book
              </i>
            </dt>
            <dd>
              Adds the currently viewed article (page) to your book.

            </dd>
            <dt>
              <i>
                Show book
              </i>
            </dt>
            <dd>
              Opens a new page which will show a list of all articles (pages) that you added to your book. On that page, you can 
              <b>
                change the order
              </b>
               of the articles in your book and 
              <b>
                structure
              </b>
               them using chapters. Further, you can 
              <b>
                download
              </b>
               the books as a 
              <a href="/wiki/PDF" title="PDF" class="mw-redirect">
                PDF
              </a>
               or 
              <a href="/wiki/OpenDocument" title="OpenDocument">
                ODF
              </a>
              , or order a 
              <b>
                printed book
              </b>
              .

            </dd>
            <dt>
              <i>
                Suggest pages
              </i>
            </dt>
            <dd>
              This tool analyzes the current set of pages in your book and 
              <b>
                suggests
              </b>
               articles that might be also relevant to the overall topic of your book. This tool allows to create books 
              <b>
                quickly
              </b>
              .

            </dd>
            <dt>
              <i>
                Disable
              </i>
            </dt>
            <dd>
              This will 
              <b>
                disable
              </b>
               the 
              <i>
                Book creator
              </i>
               and 
              <b>
                delete
              </b>
               your book (unless you saved it first).

            </dd>
          </dl>
          <h3>
            <span class="mw-headline" id="Adding_pages_without_visiting_them">
              Adding pages without visiting them
            </span>
          </h3>
          <p>
            A quick way to add pages is to simply hover on a linked article. If you wait about one second, a small box will pop up with the message "Add linked wiki page to your book". Click on this link, and the linked article will be added to your book.

          </p>
          <table>
            <tbody>
              <tr>
                <td>
                  <div class="thumb tright">
                    <div class="thumbinner" style="width:385px;">
                      <a href="/wiki/File:Collection_Extension_-_Hover_and_add.png" class="image">
                        <img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/4/4a/Collection_Extension_-_Hover_and_add.png" width="383" height="90" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="383" data-file-height="90"/>
                      </a>
                      <div class="thumbcaption">
                        <div class="magnify">
                          <a href="/wiki/File:Collection_Extension_-_Hover_and_add.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge">
                            <img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.24wmf7/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt=""/>
                          </a>
                        </div>
                        Hovering your mouse over links is a convenient way to add pages to your book
                      </div>
                    </div>
                  </div>
                </td>
              </tr>
            </tbody>
          </table>
          <h3>
            <span class="mw-headline" id="Adding_whole_categories">
              Adding whole categories
            </span>
          </h3>
          <p>
            If you are viewing a 
            <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:CATEGORY" title="Wikipedia:CATEGORY" class="mw-redirect">
              category page
            </a>
            , you can add all the pages in that category at once. The 
            <i>
              Add this page to your book
            </i>
             link will have changed into 
            <i>
              Add this category to your book
            </i>
            . Click on this new link, and all the articles in that category will be added to your book. Relevant categories may be found at the very bottom of Wikipedia articles. Categories can also be added by hovering category links.

          </p>
