                        <td style="color:#000;">
                          <div id="mp-tfa" style="padding:2px 5px">
                            <div style="float: left; margin: 0.5em 0.9em 0.4em 0em;">
                              <a href="/wiki/File:Tom_pryce_watglen_75.jpg" class="image" title="Tom Pryce racing in 1975">
                                <img alt="Tom Pryce racing in 1975" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e6/Tom_pryce_watglen_75.jpg/133px-Tom_pryce_watglen_75.jpg" width="133" height="66" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e6/Tom_pryce_watglen_75.jpg/200px-Tom_pryce_watglen_75.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e6/Tom_pryce_watglen_75.jpg/266px-Tom_pryce_watglen_75.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1849" data-file-height="924"/>
                              </a>
                            </div>
                            <p>
                              <b>
                                <a href="/wiki/Tom_Pryce" title="Tom Pryce">
                                  Tom Pryce
                                </a>
                              </b>
                               (1949–1977) was a Welsh 
                              <a href="/wiki/Racing_driver" title="Racing driver" class="mw-redirect">
                                racing driver
                              </a>
                              . He is the only Welsh driver to have won a 
                              <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">
                                Formula One
                              </a>
                               race (the 
                              <a href="/wiki/Race_of_Champions_(Brands_Hatch)" title="Race of Champions (Brands Hatch)">
                                Brands Hatch Race of Champions
                              </a>
                              , a non-championship race, in 1975) or to have led a Formula One World Championship Grand Prix (two laps of the 
                              <a href="/wiki/1975_British_Grand_Prix" title="1975 British Grand Prix">
                                1975 British Grand Prix
                              </a>
                              ). Pryce started his career in Formula One with the small 
                              <a href="/wiki/Token_Racing" title="Token Racing">
                                Token
                              </a>
                               team, making his only start for them at the 
                              <a href="/wiki/1974_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="1974 Belgian Grand Prix">
                                1974 Belgian Grand Prix
                              </a>
                              . Shortly after an impressive performance at the 
                              <a href="/wiki/Formula_Three" title="Formula Three">
                                Formula Three
                              </a>
                               support race for the 
                              <a href="/wiki/1974_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="1974 Monaco Grand Prix">
                                1974 Monaco Grand Prix
                              </a>
                              , Pryce joined the 
                              <a href="/wiki/Shadow_Racing_Cars" title="Shadow Racing Cars">
                                Shadow
                              </a>
                               team and scored his first points in 
                              <a href="/wiki/1974_German_Grand_Prix" title="1974 German Grand Prix">
                                Germany
                              </a>
                               in only his fourth race. Pryce 
                              <i>
                                (pictured during the 
                                <a href="/wiki/1975_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="1975 United States Grand Prix">
                                  1975 United States Grand Prix
                                </a>
                                )
                              </i>
                               later claimed two podium finishes: the first in 
                              <a href="/wiki/1975_Austrian_Grand_Prix" title="1975 Austrian Grand Prix">
                                Austria in 1975
                              </a>
                               and the second in 
                              <a href="/wiki/1976_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="1976 Brazilian Grand Prix">
                                Brazil a year later
                              </a>
                              . Pryce was considered by his team as a great wet weather driver, and was the fastest driver in the wet conditions of the practice session for the 
                              <a href="/wiki/1977_South_African_Grand_Prix" title="1977 South African Grand Prix">
                                1977 South African Grand Prix
                              </a>
                              , his final race. During the race, he collided at high speed with a safety marshal, Frederik Jansen van Vuuren, and both men were killed. A memorial to Pryce was unveiled in 2009 in his home town of 
                              <a href="/wiki/Ruthin" title="Ruthin">
                                Ruthin
                              </a>
                              . (
                              <a href="/wiki/Tom_Pryce" title="Tom Pryce">
                                <b>
                                  Full article...
                                </b>
                              </a>
                              )
                            </p>
