          <p>
            The 
            <b>
              1975 British Grand Prix
            </b>
             was a 
            <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">
              Formula One
            </a>
            <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" title="Motor race" class="mw-redirect">
              motor race
            </a>
             held at 
            <a href="/wiki/Silverstone_Circuit" title="Silverstone Circuit">
              Silverstone
            </a>
             on 19 July 1975. It was the 30th 
            <a href="/wiki/British_Grand_Prix" title="British Grand Prix">
              British Grand Prix
            </a>
             to be held since the race was first held in 
            <a href="/wiki/1926_British_Grand_Prix" title="1926 British Grand Prix">
              1926
            </a>
             and the 17th time the race had been held at Silverstone. The race was held over 56 of the scheduled 67 laps of the four kilometre venue for a race distance of 264 kilometres.
          </p>
          <p>
            The results were overshadowed by a heavy hail storm from Lap 53, which caused three out of the top four cars (
            <a href="/wiki/Jody_Scheckter" title="Jody Scheckter">
              Jody Scheckter
            </a>
            , 
            <a href="/wiki/James_Hunt" title="James Hunt">
              James Hunt
            </a>
            , and 
            <a href="/wiki/Mark_Donohue" title="Mark Donohue">
              Mark Donohue
            </a>
            ), to aquaplane and crash in the same corner, bringing an early finish to the race, and a significant absence on the podium. A number of other cars crashed at the same corner as well, including 
            <a href="/wiki/Wilson_Fittipaldi" title="Wilson Fittipaldi" class="mw-redirect">
              Wilson Fittipaldi
            </a>
            , 
            <a href="/wiki/Jochen_Mass" title="Jochen Mass">
              Jochen Mass
            </a>
            , and 
            <a href="/wiki/John_Watson_(racing_driver)" title="John Watson (racing driver)">
              John Watson
            </a>
            . The race results were finalised the lap after the lap most cars crashed, giving Brazilian 
            <a href="/wiki/Emerson_Fittipaldi" title="Emerson Fittipaldi">
              Emerson Fittipaldi
            </a>
            , who had been the race leader prior to the storm, a one lap win in his 
            <a href="/wiki/McLaren_M23" title="McLaren M23">
              McLaren M23
            </a>
            . 
            <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Pace" title="Carlos Pace">
              Carlos Pace
            </a>
            , who was one of the crashers in his 
            <a href="/wiki/Brabham_BT44B" title="Brabham BT44B" class="mw-redirect">
              Brabham BT44B
            </a>
             was classified in second position with another of the crashers, 
            <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tyrrell_007&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tyrrell 007 (page does not exist)">
              Tyrrell 007
            </a>
             driver 
            <a href="/wiki/Jody_Scheckter" title="Jody Scheckter">
              Jody Scheckter
            </a>
             classified third.
          </p>
          <p>
            The win was the 14th and final win of Fittipaldi's career which had included two world championships. He would continue racing in Formula One until 1980. The win also vaulted Fittpaldi past 
            <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Reutemann" title="Carlos Reutemann">
              Carlos Reutemann
            </a>
             into second place in the championship, 14 points behind Lauda.
          </p>

          <h2>
            <span class="mw-headline" id="Race_summary">
              Race summary
            </span>
            <span class="mw-editsection">
              <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
                [
              </span>
              <a href="/w/index.php?title=1975_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Race summary">
                edit
              </a>
              <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
                ]
              </span>
            </span>
          </h2>
          <p>
            A new chicane had been installed at 
            <a href="/wiki/Woodcote_Corner" title="Woodcote Corner" class="mw-redirect">
              Woodcote Corner
            </a>
            , bringing complaints from the purists but also arguments that it was necessary in the interests of safety. 
            <a href="/wiki/Tom_Pryce" title="Tom Pryce">
              Tom Pryce
            </a>
             gained his first pole position in his home race, whilst the 
            <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">
              Ferraris
            </a>
             were on the second row, with 
            <a href="/wiki/James_Hunt" title="James Hunt">
              James Hunt
            </a>
             languishing on the fifth row.
          </p>
          <p>
            <a href="/wiki/Graham_Hill" title="Graham Hill">
              Graham Hill
            </a>
             announced his retirement as a driver after 17 seasons and 176 races to concentrate on his 
            <a href="/wiki/Embassy_Hill" title="Embassy Hill">
              Embassy Hill
            </a>
             team.
          </p>
          <p>
            From the start – in which a lights system was being used for the first time in any Grand Prix, replacing the traditional national flag – 
            <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Pace" title="Carlos Pace">
              Carlos Pace
            </a>
             led from Pryce.
          </p>
          <p>
            Following the hail storm, only five cars were running at the end. The RAC declared the race finished officially on the lap after the lap when most cars were running – lap 56. 
            <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">
              Ferrari
            </a>
            , with both of their cars stuck on lap 54 in the classification protested, but the RAC threw these protests out and three days later, the provisional results were confirmed.
          </p>
