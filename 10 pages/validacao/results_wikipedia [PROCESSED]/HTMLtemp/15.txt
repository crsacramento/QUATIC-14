          <p>
            <b>
              Ibrahim Ismail Chundrigar Road
            </b>
             aka 
            <b>
              I. I. Chundrigar Road
            </b>
             is the 
            <a href="/wiki/Central_business_district" title="Central business district">
              financial district
            </a>
             of 
            <a href="/wiki/Karachi" title="Karachi">
              Karachi
            </a>
            , 
            <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">
              Pakistan
            </a>
             that is the center of Karachi's banking and financial institutions. It is the largest financial district in the country and often termed as the "
            <a href="/wiki/Wall_Street" title="Wall Street">
              Wall Street
            </a>
             of Pakistan".
            <sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference">
              <a href="#cite_note-1">
                <span>
                  [
                </span>
                1
                <span>
                  ]
                </span>
              </a>
            </sup>
             Situated in the heart of 
            <a href="/wiki/Karachi" title="Karachi">
              Karachi
            </a>
            , 
            <a href="/wiki/Sindh" title="Sindh">
              Sindh
            </a>
            , it was known as 
            <b>
              McLeod Road
            </b>
             during the Colonial rule but was later renamed after the former Prime Minister of Pakistan, 
            <a href="/wiki/Ibrahim_Ismail_Chundrigar" title="Ibrahim Ismail Chundrigar">
              Ibrahim Ismail Chundrigar
            </a>
            . The road starts from the 
            <a href="/wiki/Merewether_Memorial_Tower" title="Merewether Memorial Tower" class="mw-redirect">
              Merewether Memorial Tower
            </a>
             near Former Lloyds bank building (Now Saudi-Pak bank) up to Shaheen complex. An estimated Fifteen percent of all Karachi cars pass from this road.
          </p>
          <p>
            Most of the headquarters of Pakistan's financial institutions are located on the I. I. Chundrigar Road, including the 
            <a href="/wiki/State_Bank_of_Pakistan" title="State Bank of Pakistan">
              State Bank of Pakistan
            </a>
             which is Pakistan's central bank, major Banks like 
            <a href="/wiki/Habib_Bank_Plaza" title="Habib Bank Plaza">
              Habib Bank Plaza
            </a>
            , 
            <a href="/wiki/MCB_Tower" title="MCB Tower">
              MCB Tower
            </a>
            , 
            <a href="/wiki/National_Bank_of_Pakistan" title="National Bank of Pakistan">
              National Bank of Pakistan
            </a>
            , 
            <a href="/wiki/Bank_Al-Falah" title="Bank Al-Falah" class="mw-redirect">
              Bank Al-Falah
            </a>
            , 
            <a href="/wiki/Standard_Chartered_Bank_(Pakistan)" title="Standard Chartered Bank (Pakistan)" class="mw-redirect">
              Standard Chartered Bank
            </a>
            , 
            <a href="/wiki/CitiBank" title="CitiBank" class="mw-redirect">
              CitiBank
            </a>
            , etc. The new headquarters of 
            <a href="/wiki/United_Bank_Limited" title="United Bank Limited" class="mw-redirect">
              United Bank Limited
            </a>
             is also under construction on this road.
          </p>
          <p>
            Pakistan's most circulated newspapers including 
            <a href="/wiki/Jang_Group" title="Jang Group" class="mw-redirect">
              Jang Newspaper
            </a>
            , 
            <a href="/wiki/Daily_Times_(Pakistan)" title="Daily Times (Pakistan)">
              Daily Times
            </a>
            , TV Channels including 
            <a href="/wiki/Geo_TV" title="Geo TV">
              Geo TV
            </a>
            , 
            <a href="/wiki/CNBC_Pakistan" title="CNBC Pakistan" class="mw-redirect">
              CNBC Pakistan
            </a>
            , 
            <a href="/wiki/Hum_TV" title="Hum TV">
              Hum TV
            </a>
            , 
            <a href="/wiki/AAG_TV" title="AAG TV">
              AAG TV
            </a>
             and 
            <a href="/wiki/Dhoom" title="Dhoom">
              Dhoom
            </a>
             are also based on I.I. Chundrigar Road. Consulates of 
            <a href="/wiki/Tunisia" title="Tunisia">
              Tunisia
            </a>
            , 
            <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">
              Sweden
            </a>
            , 
            <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Leone" title="Sierra Leone">
              Sierra Leone
            </a>
            , 
            <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">
              Norway
            </a>
            , 
            <a href="/wiki/Guinea" title="Guinea">
              Guinea
            </a>
            , 
            <a href="/wiki/Gambia" title="Gambia" class="mw-redirect">
              Gambia
            </a>
             and 
            <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">
              Finland
            </a>
             are also located here.
          </p>
          <p>
            Two of Karachi's largest computer markets Uni Plaza and Techno city; Police Headquarters and nearby Rangers Headquarters are also located on this road.
          </p>
          <p>
            In 2007 Sharjah-based Enshaa Holdings in a joint venture with NLC has launched Karachi International Financial center on II Chundrigar road which will comprise a hotel, office towers, serviced apartments and a railway station. The 
            <a href="/w/index.php?title=Karachi_Financial_Towers&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Karachi Financial Towers (page does not exist)">
              Karachi Financial Towers
            </a>
             will include two identical 37-storey towers connected by a three-floor glass skybridge.
          </p>
          <p>
            I. I. Chundrigar Road has always been the financial center of Karachi's economy. Most of the biggest businesses and financial institutions in the city have offices here including the 
            <a href="/wiki/Karachi_Stock_Exchange" title="Karachi Stock Exchange">
              Karachi Stock Exchange
            </a>
            , 
            <a href="/wiki/Karachi_Cotton_Exchange" title="Karachi Cotton Exchange">
              Karachi Cotton Exchange
            </a>
            , Mutual Funds, Money Exchangers, Auditing companies, Insurance companies and Shipping companies. Karachi's largest 
            <a href="/wiki/General_Post_Office_(disambiguation)" title="General Post Office (disambiguation)">
              GPO
            </a>
             and Telegraph office is also located here. British-era architecture on this street ranges from the Former Lloyds Bank building to the 
            <b>
              <a href="/wiki/Karachi_City_Station" title="Karachi City Station" class="mw-redirect">
                City Railway Station
              </a>
            </b>
             buildings and administrative offices. This road also preserves some of the finest private architecture from Karachi's early period. World famous "Mashhood Chouk" is also located at the junction of Shara-e-Faisal and Ibrahim Ismail Chundrigar Road. It is famous for a statue of a horse, associated with historical man named Mashhood.
          </p>
